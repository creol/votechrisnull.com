What I Want to Accomplish

My priorities if elected to County Council

Reduce Taxes

I will lower taxes through responsible spending and by consolidating duplicate services under city control to eliminate double taxation.

Public Safety

I will strengthen cooperation among all levels of law enforcement to keep our county safe. Including support for cooperating with U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement to ensure safe operations.

Shrink Government

As cities grow, the county should shrink. SLCo is the county's fourth-largest employer; we must reduce bureaucracy and empower cities to lead, bringing government closer to the people.

Meet Chris Null

Experience. Leadership. Results.

A Proven Leader for Salt Lake County

Chris Null is a dedicated conservative who believes that government works best when it's closest to the people. As a longtime advocate for local control and responsible governance, Chris has been on the front lines fighting against top-down mandates that threaten our Utah way of life.

Chris is a passionate defender of election integrity and has worked with organizations like Keep My Voice to ensure that Utah voters have a real say in choosing their representatives. He believes that the caucus-convention system gives grassroots the power to hold politicians accountable.

Chris’ core beliefs begin with keeping housing affordable, protecting taxpayers through lower taxes and responsible county spending, and limiting the size and reach of government. He supports parental rights in education, public safety and the rule of law, free enterprise and small businesses, and the protection of individual liberties—including the sanctity of life and Second Amendment rights—while advocating for personal responsibility, economic opportunity, secure borders, and a strong national defense.

Mayor Trent Staggs

Former Mayor, Riverton City | Regional Advocate, U.S. Small Business Administration

I am pleased to endorse Chris Null for Salt Lake County Council, District 5. Chris understands what I've worked to prove in Riverton: government closest to the people governs best. When we created the Riverton Police Department, we returned more than $20 million to taxpayers while expanding our force by... Read More 14 officers. We did more with less because local leaders are more responsive, more accountable, and more aligned with the needs of their communities.

Chris will bring this same principle to the County Council. His vision—as city governments grow, county government must shrink—is exactly right. Salt Lake County taxpayers are currently paying twice for services their cities already provide. Parks, recreation centers, youth programs—these are better managed at the local level where leaders know their neighborhoods and answer directly to residents.

I have seen firsthand the results of fiscal discipline and innovative governance. Under my administration, Riverton has not raised taxes in nearly a decade. We have the lowest utility fees in the valley. We've done this not by cutting essential services, but by eliminating duplication, demanding accountability, and focusing limited tax dollars on what only government can do.

Chris Null shares this commitment to fiscal responsibility and taxpayer protection. At a time when the Salt Lake County Mayor proposed a nearly 20% property tax increase—the largest in decades—we need leaders who will push back on government expansion and fight for working families, seniors on fixed incomes, and small business owners who are already stretched thin.

Chris has the background to deliver. As former Chair of the Salt Lake County Republican Party and current Treasurer of the Utah Republican Party, he understands conservative governance and party platform principles. More importantly, he has the conviction to stand firm against the partisan overreach we've seen from the current county administration.

District 5 deserves a councilmember who will champion limited government, local control, and responsible stewardship of taxpayer dollars. Chris Null is that candidate. I encourage every delegate and voter in District 5 to support Chris Null for Salt Lake County Council.
